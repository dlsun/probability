Introduction to Probability

Lesson 11 Cumulative Distribution Functions

Motivating Example

Theory

Definition 11.1 (Cumulative Distribution Function) \[ F(x) = P(X \leq x) \]

Examples

  1. Continuing with the example from the lesson, let \(Y\) be the number of jacks among the community cards. Calculate and graph the c.d.f. of \(Y\).

  2. Consider a random variable \(Z\) with c.d.f. \[\begin{align*} F(z) &= \begin{cases} 1 - 3^{-\lfloor z \rfloor} & z \geq 0 \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}. \end{align*}\] Calculate:
    1. \(P(Z > 3)\)
    2. \(P(Z = 2)\)
